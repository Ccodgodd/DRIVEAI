CITIZEN PROTOCOL

KNOW YOUR RIGHTS

UNDERSTAND YOUR LEGAL RIGHTS DURING A TRAFFIC STOP UNDER THE MOTOR VEHICLES ACT.

1. OFFICER IDENTITY

Traffic police must be in uniform with their name buckle clearly visible. If they are in civilian clothes, you can ask for their ID card.

Ask for the officer's ID if not in uniform.
Don't surrender documents to an unidentified person.

2. KEY CONFISCATION

No police officer (regardless of rank) is allowed to take away your vehicle keys forcefully.

Politely refuse to hand over keys.
Don't get into physical altercations. Record a video if necessary.

3. ELECTRONIC CHALLAN

Officers must use an e-challan machine or a proper challan book. You cannot be forced to pay bribes or settle fines without an official receipt.

Insist on a printed or electronic receipt.
Never pay fine amounts directly in cash without a receipt.

4. DIGITAL DOCUMENTS

According to the IT Act and MoRTH guidelines, documents stored in DigiLocker or mParivahan are legally equivalent to physical copies.

Show your DL/RC on DigiLocker/mParivahan.
Do not hand over your phone to the officer.
